Graduation


Credits:

Every student needs 110 credits to graduate.

Each student takes 40 credits each year (8 classes of 5 credits each)

MCAS:

To receive a high school diploma, students must achieve a passing score of 220 or more on all 3 MCAS exams (Biology, English, Math). Students scoring advanced on their first try may be eligible for the John and Abigail Adams College Scholarship.

MCAS Practice Exams:

Biology (Grade 9)

English (Grade 10)

Math Session 1 (Grade 10)

Math Session 2 (Grade 10)

Spanish Math Session 1 (Grade 10)

Spanish Math Session 2 (Grade 10)

The ACCESS Test

All EL students will take the Access Test once per year in January. The ACCESS Test measures an EL student's English improvement in the domains of reading, writing, listening and speaking, and also helps teachers determine which classes will best help the student.

Students will receive a score that places them in one of these six levels:


Students are looking to improve by 1.0 on the WIDA scale each year. A perfect score of 6.0 Overall is equivalent to a native English speaker.

Entering:

  • Level 1 ESL (language and content)
  • Pre-Sheltered: US History
  • Sheltered: Biology 1
  • Sheltered: Pre-Algebra, Algebra 1, Geometry, Algebra 2
  • Mainstream: Electives

Beginning:

  • Level 2 ESL (language and content)
  • Sheltered: US History
  • Sheltered: Biology 2
  • Sheltered: Pre-Algebra, Algebra 1, Geometry, Algebra 2
  • Mainstream: Electives

Expanding:

  • Level 3 ESL (language and content)
  • Mainstream: US History 1
  • Mainstream: Chemistry or Biology of Life
  • Mainstream: Algebra 1, Geometry, Algebra 2, Pre-Calculus
  • Mainstream: Electives

Expanding:

  • Level 4 ESL (language)
  • Mainstream: English (content)
  • Mainstream: US History 2
  • Mainstream: Physics
  • Mainstream: Algebra 1, Geometry, Algebra 2, Pre-Calculus, Calculus, Statistics
  • Mainstream: Electives

FLEP:

Students have scored both a 3.9 or above in Literacy and a 4.2 or above Overall. At this point, students will no longer take ESL classes, but language support will still be provided.
